Abstract: Real-time and accurate facial dysphagia intensity classification is critical for enhancing
safety and effectiveness in human-robot interaction (HRI), especially within the context
of intelligent meal-assisting robotics. Traditional single deep neural network models,
however, often struggle to capture subtle facial changes and sequential information,
leading to inaccuracies in hierarchical dysphagia intensity classification. To address
these challenges, this paper proposes an advanced Integrated Neural Network Model
(INNM) designed for real-time detection of dysphagia intensity levels from facial video
frames. The INNM architecture comprises two key components: a feature extraction
network and a converged CNN-ConvBiLSTM network. In the feature extraction stage,
we enhance the YOLOv8s-cls architecture with deformable convolutions to better
capture fine spatial details of facial expressions. This enhancement allows for more
precise identification of subtle facial cues. Additionally, the CloFormer attention
mechanism is integrated to effectively perceive both local high-frequency and global
low-frequency information, thereby improving the model's sensitivity to nuanced facial
changes. In the converged CNN-ConvBiLSTM network, we propose a novel algorithm
that combines a three-layer hybrid CNN with ConvBiLSTM, employing a jumping-
connection strategy. This approach significantly strengthens the model's ability to
handle long-term and short-term temporal sequences, while also enhancing its
capacity to integrate and interact with spatial information. The jumping connections
improve the memorization ability of the model, making it more robust in processing
complex time-series data. Extensive experiments demonstrate that the INNM
outperforms baseline models and current state-of-the-art methods in dysphagia
intensity classification, achieving remarkable accuracy. Ablation studies further validate
the effectiveness of each component enhancement, confirming the model's superior
performance. This research not only advances the application of AI in facial expression
analysis but also provides essential technical support for ensuring safe and effective
HRI in intelligent meal-assisting robots. By integrating sophisticated neural network
models with real-time facial expression detection, our work contributes to the
development of smarter, safer robotic systems that can significantly enhance user
experience and safety in everyday applications.

1 Introduction  44 
As an emerging service robot, intelligent meal -assist ing robotics primarily consists 45 
of mechanical structure module, servo drive module, computer vision module, and 46 
communication module [1, 2]. The computer vision module is responsible for 47 
accurately recognizing and locating mouth and meal, transmitting rele vant information 48 
to the servo drive module via the communication module. Subsequently, the servo drive 49 
module controls the movement of the robotic arm to facilitate food fetching and 50 
delivery functions. Facial dysphagia intensity classification is an impor tant technology 51 
for ensuring the safety of human -robot interaction, particularly during the meal delivery 52 
phase of meal -assisting robotics. During the meal delivery process, when diners exhibit 53 
normal facial expressions, the robotic arm operates stably acc ording to predetermined 54 
program. However, if diner displays slight dysphagia expression, the arm will 55 
automatically return to initial position; in cases of moderate or severe dysphagia 56 
expressions, the arm will immediately halt all movements. Therefore, re al-time 57 
monitoring facial dysphagia intensity of diners is essential to enhance the safety of 58 
human -robot interactions in these robotic systems.  59 
Video -fluoroscopic swallowing examination [3], water drinking tests [4], and 60 
voice analysis methods [5, 6] have  shown some utility in dysphagia classification. 61 
However, these methods have significant limitations in practical applications. The 62 
monitoring and identification techniques are often influenced by subjective judgment 63 
of humans, making it challenging to ens ure objectivity and continuous real -time 64 
monitoring. This limitation significantly hampers the popularity and practicality of 65 
these methods. For instance, video -fluoroscopic swallowing examinations and water 66 
drinking tests require diagnosis by medical prof essionals, which complicates the ability 67

2.2 Proposed feature extraction  network architecture  206 
During the design process of the proposed integrated INNM model, the 207 
architecture part of the feature extraction network (Fusion 1) is designed first, as 208 
depicted in Fig. 2. The main role of the feature extraction network is to extrac t crucial 209 
feature information from each frame of the pre -processed image, which is then passed 210 
to the subsequent converged CNN -ConvBiLSTM network for further processing. For 211 
the purpose of strengthening the feature extraction ability of networks, we combin e the 212 
improved YOLOv8s -cls network with the CloFormer attention mechanism. In 213 
particular, we take YOLOv8s -cls as the baseline model for feature extraction. By 214 
introducing deformable convolutions to replace traditional convolutions, we aim to 215 
improve the mo del's ability to capture fine spatial information of face images. 216 
Simultaneously, the CloFormer attention mechanism was introduced to enhance the 217 
models' capacity to perceive local high -frequency information and global low - 218 
frequency information within the feature map. This design approach, through 219 
integrating deformable convolution in the YOLOv8s -cls network, allows the model to 220 
capture subtle changes in facial features more precisely. Meanwhile, the introduction 221 
of the CloFormer attention mechanism augment s the ability of models to perceive local 222 
and global contextual information in the feature map . 223

5 Conclusions  834 
This study aims to develop a robust facial expression analysis technique for 835 
human -robot interaction (HRI) within intelligent meal -assisting robotics, focusing on 836 
the detection of dysphagia intensity levels using an artificial intelligence appr oach. To 837 
achieve this objective, we propose an Integrated Neural Network Model (INNM) 838 
designed to automatically detect dysphagia intensity levels from facial video frames. 839 
The INNM architecture consists of two main components: a feature extraction network 840 
and a converged CNN -ConvBiLSTM network. In the feature extraction stage, we 841 
optimized the backbone of the YOLOv8s -cls model by substituting classical 842 
convolutions with deformable convolutions, significantly enhancing the model's ability 843 
to capture fine spa tial details of facial expressions. Additionally, the CloFormer 844 
attention mechanism was introduced to improve the model's capacity to perceive both 845 
local high -frequency and global low -frequency information, thereby increasing its 846 
sensitivity to nuanced fac ial changes. In the converged CNN -ConvBiLSTM network, 847 
we developed a novel algorithm that integrates a three -layer hybrid CNN with 848 
ConvBiLSTM, leveraging a jumping -connection strategy. This approach effectively 849 
strengthens the model's memorization ability in handling long -term and short -term 850 
temporal sequences while enhancing its capability to integrate and interact with spatial 851 
information. The jumping connections further improve the model's robustness in 852 
processing complex time -series data. To validate th e performance of the INNM model, 853 
extensive experiments were conducted on a large facial dysphagia dataset. These 854 
experiments not only compared our model against baseline models but also 855 
comprehensively evaluated it against single neural network models and current state - 856
